Cast Iron Dutch Oven Bread

by J. Kenji Lopez-Alt

Based on Jim Lahey's recipe from 2006

Ingredients

Measuring by weight instead of volume will give the most consistent results!

  • 400 grams bread flour
  • 8 gram salt
  • 2 grams instant yeast
  • 280 grams warm water
  • 1/8 tsp white vinegar or lemon juice
  • extra flour for dusting

Instructions

  1. Mix the dough: Combine the flour, salt and yeast in a large bowl and mix with your hands until mostly homogenous. Combine the water and vinegar or lemon juice, then add to the bowl.
  2. Scrape your dough-covered hand with your clean hand or with a metal or plastic dough scraper to get most of the dough into the bowl, then invert a tall-sided medium metal or glass bowl (or a cutting board) and place it on top of the large bowl.
  3. Let dough rest at least 12 hours and up to 18 at room temperature (60 to 70 degrees)
  4. Shape the loaf.
  5. Proof the loaf. Cover and the dough ball to rise until it roughly doubles in size, about two hours.
  6. Heat the oven at least 30 min before baking with the cast iron dutch inside.

Many people find kneading overwhelming, so being able to make bread and skip that step will be appealing.  This makes a nice rustic, chewy bread that is delicious in many applications from sandwiches, grilled cheese, a side for pasta with some garlic and butter and much more!
